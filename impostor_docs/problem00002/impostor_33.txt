Acelain raised an eyebrow as he straightened up to push on the side of the asset’s head. The asset leaned away, flinching. “Answer, 1066.” The asset bit the inside of his cheek before he spat, a thick red liquid landing on the floor. “That’s sixty-five seconds, project Aranal Prime, condition 3.” Acelain hummed, and wrote something down in flowing text. And then turned glacial eyes on the asset.

 “Get dressed,” Acelain commanded, and not for the first time the asset wished he had mind blankers, something to keep the thoughts from the project. Anything to keep Acelain out.

 But the asset got up without a word, putting on the clothing that he was indicated toward. It was loose, which meant he wasn’t expected to fight. Not that he would.
